FEATURED September 2025

One Day of Work Per Week Sufficient for Mental Health Benefits

Our groundbreaking research, conducted with the University of Cambridge, has redefined understanding of the relationship between work and wellbeing. The study of over 70,000 UK residents reveals that just 8 hours of work per week provides the full mental health benefits of employment.

Key Finding:

Moving from unemployment to just 8 hours of paid work per week reduces mental health problems by 30% - with no additional benefits from working more hours.

Published in Social Science & Medicine

Policy Implications:

  • ▸ Challenges the 40-hour workweek standard
  • ▸ Supports redistribution of work in automated economies
  • ▸ Informs universal basic income debates
  • ▸ Guides mental health and employment policies
Read more at Cambridge University
POLICY UPDATE December 2024

Singapore Implements Tripartite Guidelines on Flexible Work Arrangement Requests (TG-FWAR)

Singapore has officially implemented nationwide guidelines enabling employees to formally request flexible work arrangements — marking a major shift in workplace policy and support for work-life balance.

Key Requirements:

  • • Employees may formally request FWAs
  • • Employers must consider requests fairly
  • • Employers must respond within 2 months
  • • Reasons needed only when rejecting on valid business grounds

Guidance for Employers:

  • ▸ Create clear FWA request procedures and communicate them to staff
  • ▸ Ensure workflows support flexible arrangements productively
  • ▸ Use fair and consistent evaluation standards

Policy by: Ministry of Manpower, NTUC & SNEF

Took effect 1 December 2024

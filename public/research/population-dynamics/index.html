NEW RESEARCH Public Health • 2025

The role of loneliness in mediating the relationship between financial strain and mental health

A UK longitudinal analysis with gender differences

Lei Chai • Senhu Wang • Zhuofei Lu

Key Finding

Loneliness mediates the effect of financial strain on mental distress, with women showing stronger vulnerability — highlighting the need for gender-responsive public health strategies.

Using six waves of UKHLS data (2017–2023, N=34,535), this study shows that financial strain increases loneliness and worsens mental health, particularly among women.

NEW RESEARCH RSSM • 2025

Work-schedule instability and workers’ health and well-being across socioeconomic strata in China

Evidence from the Chinese labour market

Ya Guo • Wanying Ling • Wen Fan • Senhu Wang

Key Finding

Work-schedule instability harms health and job satisfaction across both low- and high-SES workers, mediated by work-family conflict and work intensification.

Using CGSS 2021 data, this study highlights schedule instability as a growing public health and labour concern in China’s digital and high-pressure work culture.

Our Research Areas

GPHA advances knowledge through four interconnected research pillars, addressing critical challenges in population health, migration, inequality, and social transformation to inform evidence-based policies and interventions globally.

Population Dynamics and Social Transformation

Understanding demographic shifts and their impact on society, labor markets, and family structures

Key Research Topics

  • • Demographic Transitions: Aging societies, fertility decline, and population structure changes
  • • Labor Market Evolution: Future of work, gig economy, and changing employment patterns
  • • Family Dynamics: Modern family structures, work-life balance, and gender roles
  • • Urbanization: Rural-urban migration and megacity development
  • • Digital Transformation: Technology's impact on social connections

Migration, Integration and Global Mobility

Examining migration patterns, integration processes, and the experiences of mobile populations

Key Research Topics

  • • Migration Patterns: International and internal migration flows and drivers
  • • Integration Pathways: Social, economic, and cultural integration of migrants
  • • Diaspora Networks: Transnational connections and remittance flows
  • • Refugee Protection: Forced displacement and humanitarian responses
  • • Brain Circulation: Skilled migration and knowledge transfer

Health Inequality and Society

Addressing disparities in health outcomes and understanding social determinants of health

Key Research Topics

  • • Social Determinants: Income, education, and housing impacts on health
  • • Mental Health Disparities: Access to care and treatment outcomes
  • • Environmental Justice: Unequal exposure to environmental health risks
  • • Healthcare Access: Barriers to quality healthcare services
  • • Life Course Health: Cumulative disadvantage and health trajectories

Social Policy and Interventions

Developing and evaluating evidence-based policies and programs for population wellbeing

Key Research Topics

  • • Policy Evaluation: Impact assessment of social and health policies
  • • Intervention Design: Evidence-based program development and testing
  • • Welfare Systems: Social protection and safety net effectiveness
  • • Community Programs: Local-level interventions and grassroots initiatives
  • • Policy Innovation: Behavioral insights and nudge interventions
